Student Welfare Association Policies

Guidelines, regulations, and terms for all SWA members

Policy Overview

This page outlines the official policies, guidelines, and regulations governing the Student Welfare Association (SWA). All members are expected to familiarize themselves with and adhere to these policies. These policies are designed to ensure fair, transparent, and effective operations of our association.

Last Updated: March 2025 | Version: 1.0

1. Membership Policies

1.1 Eligibility

All registered students of Jaramogi Oginga Odinga University of Science and Technology (JOOUST) are eligible to become members of the Student Welfare Association. This includes:

  • Full-time undergraduate students
  • Part-time students
  • Postgraduate students
  • Students in any year of study
  • Students from all faculties and departments

1.2 Membership Registration

To become an active member, students must:

  • Complete the official registration form (available at the Student Portal)
  • Provide a valid student ID card for verification
  • Provide accurate personal and contact information
  • Agree to abide by the association's constitution and policies
  • Submit a passport-sized photograph

Note: Registration is FREE for all students. There is no membership fee to join SWA.

1.3 Membership Rights

Active members have the right to:

  • Vote and hold office in general elections
  • Access all SWA services and programs
  • Apply for loans and financial assistance
  • Attend general meetings and participate in discussions
  • Receive welfare support when in need
  • Access the member portal and resources

1.4 Membership Obligations

Members are required to:

  • Make monthly contributions as agreed upon
  • Attend general meetings when summoned
  • Adhere to the code of conduct
  • Participate in association activities when possible
  • Update their contact information promptly
  • Maintain good standing with the association

2. Contribution Policies

2.1 Monthly Contributions

Members are required to make monthly contributions to support the association's operations and welfare programs. Key points:

  • Minimum Amount: Ksh 500 per month (determined each semester by the executive committee)
  • Due Date: 5th of each month
  • Grace Period: 10 days after the due date
  • Contributions can be increased voluntarily

2.2 Payment Methods

We accept payments through the following methods:

  • M-Pesa: Use the paybill number provided in the member portal
  • Bank Transfer: Direct deposit to SWA's designated account
  • Cash Payment: Pay directly at the SWA office during office hours

All contributions must be recorded. Members should:

  • Keep the M-Pesa transaction confirmation message
  • Obtain a receipt for cash payments
  • Upload payment proof to the member portal
  • Check their contribution history regularly

3. Loan Guidelines

3.1 Loan Eligibility

To qualify for a loan, members must meet the following requirements:

  • Be an active member for at least 3 months
  • Have at least 3 months of continuous contribution history
  • Have no outstanding loans
  • Have a good standing (no disciplinary issues)
  • Provide a guarantor who is also an active member

3.2 Loan Limits

Loans are subject to the following limits:

  • Minimum Loan: Ksh 500
  • Maximum Loan: 3 times the member's total contributions
  • First-time borrowers: Maximum of Ksh 5,000
  • Repeat borrowers: Based on contribution history and repayment record

3.3 Interest Rates

Our loans offer competitive interest rates:

  • Interest Rate: 5% per month on reducing balance
  • This is significantly lower than commercial lenders
  • Interest is calculated on the remaining principal balance

3.4 Repayment Terms

Loan repayment guidelines:

  • Repayment Period: 6 months maximum
  • Repayment Method: Monthly installments via M-Pesa or bank transfer
  • Early Repayment: Allowed without penalty
  • Late Payment: Additional 2% interest on overdue amounts

3.5 Default Consequences

Failure to repay loans may result in:

  • Suspension of membership privileges
  • Withholding of academic documents
  • Legal action in severe cases
  • Reporting to university administration

4. Code of Conduct

4.1 Core Principles

All members are expected to uphold the following principles:

  • Respect: Treat all members with dignity and respect
  • Integrity: Act honestly and ethically in all dealings
  • Collaboration: Work together for the common good
  • Responsibility: Take ownership of actions and commitments
  • Confidentiality: Protect sensitive information when required

4.2 Prohibited Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

  • Harassment or discrimination of any kind
  • Misappropriation of association funds
  • Sharing false or misleading information
  • Disruption of association activities
  • Violation of university regulations

4.3 Disciplinary Actions

Violations may result in:

  • Warning letters (written or verbal)
  • Suspension of membership privileges
  • Suspension from holding office
  • Termination of membership
  • Legal action where applicable

5. Member Benefits

Active members of SWA are entitled to the following benefits:

  • Interest-Free Loans: Access affordable financial support
  • Emergency Welfare Support: Assistance during crises
  • Academic Support: Tutoring and study resources
  • Career Development: Workshops and networking opportunities
  • Networking Events: Connect with fellow students and alumni
  • Community Service: Volunteer opportunities
  • Leadership Opportunities: Serve on committees and executive positions
  • Member Discounts: Partnerships with local businesses

6. Privacy & Data Protection

6.1 Information We Collect

We collect personal information necessary for membership management, including:

  • Full name and contact details
  • Student ID and academic information
  • Financial contribution records
  • Loan application and repayment history
  • Emergency contact information

6.2 How We Use Your Information

Your information is used for:

  • Membership administration and communication
  • Processing loan applications and contributions
  • Providing welfare support services
  • Sending notifications about events and programs
  • Maintaining accurate financial records

6.3 Data Protection

We are committed to protecting your personal information:

  • Your data is stored securely and access is restricted
  • We do not share your information with third parties without consent
  • You have the right to access and update your information
  • Data is retained only as long as necessary

7. Grievance Policy

7.1 Filing a Complaint

Members who have concerns or complaints should:

  • First attempt to resolve the issue informally
  • Submit a formal complaint in writing to the Secretary
  • Include all relevant details and evidence
  • Expect a response within 14 working days

7.2 Appeal Process

Unsatisfied parties may appeal to:

  • The Executive Committee for initial decisions
  • The General Assembly for final resolution
